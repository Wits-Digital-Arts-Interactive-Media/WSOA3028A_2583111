Essay 1 - UX / UI analysis

  • Essay 1: UX and UI Analysis of Takealot’s Website Design

    • User interface (UI) and user experience (UX) are fundamental components of online interactions, as they directly impact how users interact and engage with them. Every digital product has a goal set out and a way of executing this goal through the interactions and engagement of the user. Analysing these goals is one part of this analysis, and to do so, I will use the website Takealot as an example. Websites like Takealot, a major e-commerce platform, heavily rely on these interactions, so they must have a responsive, engaging, and satisfying UI and UX design. Through this analysis, I want to examine the design elements and how effectively they have been executed.

    Takealot, being an e-commerce website, has specific goals: to provide a convenient and efficient online marketplace where customers can purchase a wide range of products. To do that, the UI is designed for intuitive navigation through the site and effective browsing of the products available as “User Interface (UI), in other words User Interface would be the first thing that users meet. User Interface also can be defined as the first impression that the user meets.” (Ricky Gunawan, Geraldi Anthony, Vendly and Maria Susan Anggreainy, 2021). The homepage displays a simple layout, featuring categorization of products called “Departments” on the left screen, a promotional banner with featured brands, and a search bar that is easily accessible to reach desired items.

    The promotion of the products to the user through the promotional banner is a micro transaction that happens within the website, and this helps with “purchase intention,” as seeing this might push users to further look into the product, pushing this ‘intention’ to purchase it, “Individuals have always been speculating that useful design can trigger their Purchase Intention, to make Purchase Intention is depending on good User Interface is, that including Visual Design, Micro-Interaction and Design Layout.” (Ricky Gunawan, Geraldi Anthony, Vendly and Maria Susan Anggreainy, 2021). The layout is visually clean, with appealing images and concise text describing the products, which helps facilitate quick decision-making for users. The use of simple colors is important as it helps with identifying each option given to the user. With the layout being white and blue with their branding on the top right left, the use of reds for the wish list and greens for the cart keeps the layout visually concise while users navigate the website.

    “Colour has a range of culturally dependent significances and thus it is very important to take this into consideration when designing multilingual and multiregional websites.” (Mahdi H. Miraz, Peter S. Excell, and Maaruf Ali, 2017). The inclusion of filters to sort options on the category pages allows users to refine their search results based on their preferences.

    When looking at interactivity, Takealot uses features such as recommending products and brands, allowing reviews from customers, having related items under the current product you are looking at, and adding additional information such as descriptions of products. The checkout process is streamlined with an easy step-by-step flow, which enhances the user experience as there is no frustration when interacting with this system, and this also plays a part in the user experience (UX). Overall, the UI design prioritizes simplicity, functionality, and aesthetics, contributing to a positive user experience.

    The UX revolves around delivering a seamless and more personalized shopping experience to create an emotion between the user and the system, “The entire set of affects that is elicited by the interaction between a user and a product including the degree to which all our senses are gratified (aesthetic experience) the meanings we attach to the product (experience of meaning) and the feelings and emotions that are elicited (emotional experience)” (A. H. Allam, Ab Razak Che Hussin and Halina Mohamed Dahlan, 2013), this highlights that user experience encompasses more than just usability and functionality; it includes users' feelings and emotions, indicating the importance of personalization in creating a positive experience. Takealot uses an algorithm that recommends products to the consumer based on past search history and purchase behaviour, which increases the personality of a specific user and encourages repeat visits. Features like the wish list and order tracking allow players to manage their shopping activities efficiently.

    The UX design also focuses on trust and reliability, with clear communication of product details, pricing, and delivery information. Takealot provides customer support by allowing access to FAQs and customer service channels, which ensures assistance is readily available to the user when needed. “When it comes to 'Evaluation of User Experience', it is to support and help in selecting the best design, to make sure the development is on right track, or to measure and assess whether the final product meets and comply with the original UX targets [13]. There is a need to enrich and have a holistic perspective of traditional usability models to include non-functional concepts such as joy, fun and pleasure. At the same time, literature review on experiential marketing has shown and stressed that a product should not only be considered as providing a set of functional features and benefits, instead it should provide experiences.” (A. H. Allam, Ab Razak Che Hussin and Halina Mohamed Dahlan, 2013), this implies that part of ensuring a positive user experience involves providing support and assistance to users, which aligns with Takealot's provision of customer support channels such as FAQs and customer service. The responsive design of Takealot ensures that there is consistent performance across various platforms, such as PCs, smartphones, and tablets, catering to the needs of user accessibility.

    In summary, Takealot's website design exemplifies the pivotal role of user interface (UI) and user experience (UX) in shaping online interactions. Through intuitive navigation, visually appealing layouts, and streamlined features, Takealot provides a seamless and personalized shopping experience. The UI design emphasizes simplicity, functionality, and aesthetics, facilitating quick decision-making for users with clear communication and interactive elements. Meanwhile, the UX design focuses on creating emotional connections through personalized recommendations, order tracking, and reliable customer support channels like FAQs. By prioritizing trust, reliability, and accessibility across various platforms, Takealot ensures consistent performance and user satisfaction. Overall, Takealot's integrated approach to UI and UX design underscores how digital platforms can optimize online interactions, enhance user engagement, and foster long-term customer loyalty in the competitive e-commerce landscape.
